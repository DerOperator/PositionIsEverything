This demo shows what happens in Op6 and Op7 when a long series of floats are forced to wrap against an opposing float.

The grey boxes (div.leftfloat) are all left floated, and the green box (div.rightfloat) is right floated. The orange container (div.floatholder) is a static element, with no specified 'width'. That's really all there is to it.

In Op6, when the advancing line of left floats reaches the green right float, the next line waits until the right float will no longer interfere, before rendering. Op7 is better, allowing two lines of floats to properly render before its bug forces the rest below the green float.

If the float directions are reversed, the demo behaves identically in Op6, but Op7 strangely starts behaving correctly. Weird.

Only Opera has this bug. Mozilla renders correctly, and even IE will get it right if the container has a specified 'width'.

"But you said this demo had no width!"
That's right. So if you choose to view this in IE, don't say I didn't warn you.

Update, March 29, 2005: Opera 8.b3 appears to have finally vanquished this long lived bug. The demo now appears exactly as does Gecko in that beta Opera version. Woohoo!

Kudos to Thomas (AKA "Tomcat76" on the Opera Support Forums) for the timely heads up.
